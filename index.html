About Me

I solve problems one pixel and one bracket at a time. Currently I’m the UX/Visual Designer at Upsight. I lead the user experience strategy at Upsight and design new product features from specs to wireframes to full mockups. I helped design and code the Upsight website. I also work on the product marketing for print and web. Since Upsight merged and rebranded into a new company in Nov 2013, I helped rebrand Upsight, redesign our current dashboard/product, the new brand and UI style guides. 

Before Upsight, I worked on my own startup, Matchpuppy while I worked at OMGPOP. At OMGPOP, I helped design UI elements for DrawSomething, designed the marketing campaign for the game and worked on marketing ads for other games, as well as, UI game elements. At Matchpuppy, I designed the website and web app for dog owners to meet other dog owners. I was inspired by my dogs lack of social skills and my cofounder and I built the startup. It was an interesting learning experience. We grew our user base to 10,000 users, we didn’t receive any funding, so we stopped the project.

Before Matchpuppy and OMGPOP, I got my first job that jumpstarted my career in design. I didn’t have a portfolio or design background. I received a BS in Actuarial Science with a minor in Art History. My first job out of college was a risk analyst at a mid sized vision insurance firm in New York. I knew I wanted to design because I was always obsessed with HTML/CSS and drawing. From the age of 11, I was convinced that everyone needed a website. I made a geocities websites for my mom, for my dad and for my dog. 

I left my job pursuing actuarial science and enrolled in design classes at the School of Visual Arts in New York. My first design job was at 20th Street Media, a small design firm in New York City. I applied for a job there and begged them to hire me for no pay. I didn’t have Photoshop/Illustrator knowledge, but I did have some typography design projects in my portfolio that I worked on at SVA. They didn’t exemplify any computer design knowledge, but it showed that I had a knack for creativity. I got the job. Three months later and 400 hours of photoshop and illustrator tutorials later, I was hired. I worked on any website that came through the door.
